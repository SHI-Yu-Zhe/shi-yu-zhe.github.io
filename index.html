Currently I'm insterested in the reciprocative research of Machine Learning and Cognitive Science, where the former is promoted by the latter, to find a way towards Artificial General Intelligence that solves high-level tasks with human-level intelligence. Though enjoying a boost from early 2010s with the rapid progress of computing power, deep-learning based industrial applications of artificial intelligence suffers from the uncomprehensibility of the model, thus arises unreliability. Also, the black-box method is also limited by its data-hungry nature and the capacity to handle high-level cognitive learning tasks. Hence, deep-learning is obviously not the response to the call for general intelligence. As individuals with high-level intelligence, human intelligence provides a infinite source of templates for machine intelligence. Understanding the mental process of human abstraction, planning, decision and inventing new concepts absolutely shed light on machine learning research.
